15 minutes between Gondomar and Porto

Poster

2021

scroll down

keyboard_arrow_down

This project aims to visually encapsulate a shared media ecology within a shared universe: the transitional space between the domestic and the academic – the journey home-university and university-home. Within this context, we used the radio as a starting point for the mapping of our media ecology, as it is a technology that is extremely prevalent on a car ride. Moreover, we valued the notion of white noise associated with the radio. Made with Processing.

In collaboration with Ana Isabel Teixeira.

Shipwreck at the Treshold of Europe

Editorial

2021

scroll down

keyboard_arrow_down

EU's handling of migrants and refugees is a prepetual failure. As European citizens, we're fed a distorted glimpse of their journeys by the media, as to avoid the risk of steering us to empathise and take real action. We can't accept their truth and must call for accountability and action. This essay is a reminder of what's concealed from us.

In collaboration with Ana Isabel Teixeira and Inês Canha.

BPI

Identity

2020

scroll down

keyboard_arrow_down

BPI is a Portuguese bank with its own personality. The experience gained through the years is combined with harmony, security and stability to form a solid and memorable identity that establishes a trustworthy brand. The symbol created evokes two elements that belong to BPI's universe: the vault – representative of an idea of security; and the orange blossom, which accompanies the bank since its foundation and represents concepts such as dedication, sense of service and hospitality. The first element, in a direct and immediate reading; the second, in a later analysis.

Max Bill Exhibition

Identity

2020

scroll down

keyboard_arrow_down

Max Bill is a graphic and industrial designer, artist, architect and sculptor. Born in Switzerland in 1908, he was one of the most influential Swiss designers. A precursor of the concrete art movement and an advocate of the idea that art could be produced based exclusively on mathematical thinking. This project aims to build a visual identity for a fictional design exhibition of his works.

Desassossego

Poster

2019

scroll down

keyboard_arrow_down

Desassossego is a typographic poster that illustrates the semantic and etymological value of the word "disquiet", conveying the very feeling that the word carries, through the scribbles that extend across the poster and the abundant use of exclamation and question marks. The poster was designed as a fold-out, divided into four equal parts, which works both folded and as a whole. This idea is reinforced by the small texts found in each corner, containing the definition of the word, synonyms, related words and etymology.
